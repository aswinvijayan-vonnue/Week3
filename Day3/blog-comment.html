The Necessity of Hard Work

July 30, 2020

Throughout my life, I've experienced setbacks, whether they were in sports, academics, or any other extracurriculars, due to my lack of raw talent in an activity. Ever since I can remember, I have had a competitive spirit and have always wanted to be the best, regardless of how long it took to perfect my craft. I have this permanent mindset reminding me day after day that, “hard work pays off.” Whenever I get worn out or tired of something I am practicing or working towards, I tell myself that in the end, all my efforts and sacrifices will be worth it.

In my first year of high school, I began the year with an attitude revolving around improving my level of hard work to achieve success through marks.

have never been the type of student who could naturally look at a math unit and know everything before it is even taught or look at a new concept in science and understand it within minutes.

Blatantly, I am not someone who is naturally gifted in almost everything academic. I am someone who needs to spend hours upon hours doing every question of the math or science homework and still go in for extra help to ask about a certain concept. In class, although some students may debate asking a teacher a question when they are unclear, I am the student who simply raises my hand and asks a question without hesitation.

My hard work correlates to who I am as a person and the ideas of determination, perseverance, and motivation surround my daily life. As my commitment to hard work continues to strengthen, so does my competitive mentality. This has allowed me to maintain a sense of healthy competition with my peers, motivating me to obtain the best mark in class. Not only is this additional motivation applied to my studies, but it instills a purpose towards my goals and reasons behind my countless hours of studying.

In the span of my adolescent life, I have never seen myself as the most talented person in any activity. I have always understood that there is always someone with more capabilities than me. However, the difference between me and everyone else, is that I will do anything to become the best version of myself. I will outwork my competition and do whatever it takes to improve whichever skill is at hand and persevere through adversity.

At a tryout, we rise to the occasion and perfect our craft, ensuring that we showcase the best possible version of ourselves. My belief is that if I treat everything like a tryout, it will allow me to always be my best self and never be short of my potential.

All-in-all, hard work has had an incredible impact on my perception of living and is a staple in my life that I will forever rely on. A strong work ethic is the difference between being average and being incredible, which is why it is a necessity to persevere through hardship and remain resilient.

Author: Jack Hetherington

Happy Accidents

September 9, 2018

My work deals with chance in a couple of different ways. I walk around Los Angeles and take pictures of the people that I paint. When I get a good photograph or a good character, I make the image into a painting. How I find these people to paint is completely up to the fates or chance. I just walk around, and whoever comes across my path that I would like to paint, I will paint.

My work also has to do with chance or happy accidents because I let the process of painting and intuition take over my work when I am doing it. If a drip happens with the spray paint, (I don't plan it), I just use it as a part of the painting or layer of the painting, and then see what the next layer will bring.

The work has to do with people that stand out to me, and as a body of work, the characters fit together as a whole. The characters also fit together by chance. I am including some more writing about my work so you can understand it more.

Who are you? What do you do? Why do you do it?

I am a teacher, and a painter. I have been a painter all my life because I love looking at paintings and trying to understand them and want to create my own.

My work is always about human life: portraits, full figures or partial figures. I usually paint psychics, gypsies, musicians or homeless people. My work is done in unrealistic colors. I work from photograph, and use my imagination, intuition, and fantasy to embellish the painting. I want to create an image of a mystical person in a mystical world. I aim to create a world oscillating between dreams and reality. I want to paint the person's aura, soul, energy, or situation in life. My pictures are both light and dark showing both the light and dark aspect of the person and their relation to the world. I am influenced by Tarot Cards, Surrealism, Expressionism, Bay Area Figurative, Beat Artists, and Abstract Expressionism.

What is the relationship between creativity and synchronicity?

Synchronicity refers to events that appear related but have no connection. I guess it seems more like fate, chance or accidents. Creativity is related to synchronicity, partially because when I paint, I use spray paint and it drips, and I use accidents to create the picture. The accidents are not planned, and I leaved them on the surface and use them with other layers to create the final piece. I also use my intuition to create the painting. I work from photograph with an image in mind, but let intuition flow through my body to create the image. That is somewhat synchronicity.

Author: Katie McGuire

Revelations

January 20, 2019

I looked back at my body of work over many years and noticed a theme: undeniable feminine symbolism in the form of flowers, moons, water. Feminine energy was unconsciously flowing through my artwork. Meanwhile, in my day-to-day life, I was a complete hard-ass. What a disconnect

We each carry a mix of masculine and feminine energies. It has nothing to do with gender. Masculine is the active force, the sun, utility and drive. Feminine energy is the moon, open, receiving, intuitive. It's the gentle power that nurtures. Ideally, we cultivate a balance of both (the right and left hemispheres of our brain: our temple). The result of balance is wisdom: when masculine action marries feminine knowing.

My learned behaviors had knocked me off center. Experience had taught me that vulnerability invited abuse, so I believed that showing emotion was dangerous. I didn't feel safe unless I was grasping control of every situation and person around me. What if they left? What if they were lying to me? My mind was a vigilant sentry

My attempts at control had me spread way beyond my own boundaries. Constant monitoring had kept me safe for so long - it's all I knew - but my hypervigilance was giving me chronic migraines. My life lacked spontaneity and every relationship was just a re-run of the last.Something had to change.

The feminine energy that oozed out of me in symbolism saved me. The vulnerable side of myself had survived and was crying out to be freed. Through journaling and meditation, I excavated events that had caused me to bury my emotions so long ago. I recognized my learned coping mechanisms and I forgave my history of poor choices.

As a child, when your parents abandon you, you tend to abandon yourself. I had been taught to abandon my needs and make others the focus of my life, as if I wasn't even a real person. As if I had no value.

Author: Greta Latchford
